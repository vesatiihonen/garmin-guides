Fuel & Hydration

Two jobs at once: lose weight (→ 70–71 kg) and hit the two quality sessions fully fuelled. The trick is timing and periodising, not just eating less — carbs around the hard work, the deficit from the easy days, protein and fluid every day.

The one rule: fuel for the work required. Carbs go up on quality/long days (you can't lose weight if you can't do the session that builds the engine) and down on easy/rest days (where the fat loss quietly happens). Protein and hydration stay high every day.

Daily rhythm

~136 g protein · 4 feeds
On waking
0.5 L water + a pinch of salt (overnight you dehydrate). Coffee is fine — it's not the dehydrator it's rumoured to be.
Pre-AM run
Easy/short: run fasted is fine. Long or quality: a small carb hit (banana, toast+honey, ~30–40 g) 20–40 min before.
Post-session
Within 30–60 min: ~30 g protein + carbs (bigger after quality/long). This is the recovery window — don't skip it to "save calories".
Lunch
Protein ~30–40 g + veg + carbs sized to the day (more on a hard day). Your biggest carb meal sits nearest the session.
Afternoon
Protein snack if there's a gap >4 h (Greek yoghurt, quark, skyr). Keeps muscle protected in the deficit.
Dinner
Protein + veg-forward; carbs light on easy/rest days. Finish ≥3 h before bed — late heavy meals wreck deep sleep, your #1 limiter.
Evening
Hungry late? Casein-type protein (quark/cottage cheese) or a small light snack — not a second dinner. Caffeine cut-off ~8 h before bed.

Fuel by day type

carb periodisation
Quality / long day

Carbs UP

  • Carbs before the session, and during if it's >90 min (30–60 g/h — gel, sports drink, dates).
  • Protein + carbs after, promptly. Refill the tank you just used.
  • Total carbs ~4–6 g/kg on these days (~300–450 g). This is not the day to cut.
  • Eat to hit the session; a blunted quality day is a wasted week.
Easy / rest day

Carbs DOWN

  • Carbs ~2–3 g/kg, weighted to earlier in the day. This is where the deficit lives.
  • Protein stays high (~136 g) — muscle is protected by protein, not by carbs.
  • Fat fills the rest: olive oil, nuts, fish, eggs. Satiating, sleep-friendly.
  • Easy runs can be run lower-carb / partly fasted to nudge fat adaptation.

Hydration

Garmin tracks the goal + sweat

Daily target

Garmin goal ~3–4 L (auto-adjusts for load + heat)

  • Log intake on the watch (hold the upper button → Hydration) so the goal/sweat data means something.
  • Steady sips all day beats chugging. Urine pale straw = on track; dark = behind.
  • Weigh before/after a big session if unsure: 1 kg lost ≈ 1 L to replace.

Around a session

pre · during · post

  • Pre: 0.3–0.5 L in the 2 h before; top up right before an AM session.
  • During: 0.4–0.8 L/h when hot or >60 min; sip, don't flood.
  • Post: replace ~150% of what you lost over the next few hours, with sodium.
In heat (and unacclimatised): sweat and sodium losses jump. Add electrolytes (500–1000 mg sodium per litre) whenever sweat >1 L or it's hot, train early, pre-cool with cold fluid, and judge effort by HR/feel, not pace. Salt your food generously — in a deficit + heat you are not over-salted.

Sleep, alcohol & the deficit

where it's won or lost

Protect sleep

  • Last big meal ≥3 h before bed; late digestion cuts deep sleep.
  • Caffeine cut-off ~8 h before target bedtime.
  • Consistent bedtime beats total hours — the single highest-ROI change you can make.

Alcohol & social

  • A drink with friends is fine — just know it suppresses deep sleep and recovery and stalls fat loss for that night.
  • Alternate each drink with water; add electrolytes before bed.
  • Never the night before a quality session or a long run.

Your numbers: protein ≈ 1.8 g/kg body weight, spread across ~4 feeds; the daily calorie target arrives in the coach email (TDEE − 550, softer − 250 on recovery weeks); hydration ~3–4 L, more in heat.

Why timing beats "eat less": carbs around quality keep the sessions sharp, protein spread keeps muscle in the deficit, and the calorie gap comes from the easy days — so you lose fat and the engine still grows. Each kg off ≈ +37 m of Cooper distance.

Track it: log water on the watch and step on the scale weekly (from Aug 1) — the coach reads both and steers from the trend.
