Before every run · ~8 minutes · no equipment

Run · Warm-Up

Insurance for the three past-injury sites: hamstring, Achilles, knee. Do the nine moves in this order — warm the tissue, swing it through range, switch the muscles on, prime the tendon springs, then run. Skipping this is how a hamstring seizes at 15 km.

Never static-stretch cold. Long stretch holds belong after the run (gentle, 30 s). Before the run everything is moving — momentum, not holds.

1 · Warm

~2 min
01

Brisk walk → march

2 min · arms swinging · pick up the pace each 30 s

raises tissue temperature — everything after works better warm

  • Start easy, finish at a power-walk with high arms.
  • Cold morning or pre-quality day: make it 3–4 min or add 60 s easy rope skips.
▶ "dynamic warm-up march"

2 · Swing — hamstrings & hips

~2.5 min
02

Leg swings, front–back

10 / leg · hold a fence/tree · grow the arc

dynamic hamstring length — the #1 move for your hamstrings

  • Tall spine; swing from the hip, leg loose like a pendulum.
  • Start small, let the arc grow — never force the top.

✕ kicking to max height from swing one

▶ "leg swings warm-up"
03

Leg swings, side–side

10 / leg · across the body and out

hips + adductors control knee tracking — knee protection

  • Face the support, swing the leg across and away.
  • Hips stay square; the leg crosses, the torso doesn't twist.
▶ "lateral leg swings"
04

Hamstring scoops

8 / leg · walking · heel out, hinge, scoop

loads the hamstring gently through its full length

  • Step heel out on a straight leg, toe up; hinge at the hip and "scoop" the air past your foot.
  • Feel a gentle pull behind the thigh — pull, never pain.

✕ rounding the back to reach lower

▶ "hamstring scoops"

3 · Activate — glutes & knees

~1.5 min
05

Hinge + reach (single leg)

8 / leg · slow · hips square

wakes glutes + hamstring together; teaches the hinge, not the back

  • Soft knee on the standing leg; free leg drifts back as you tip.
  • Reach toward the ground, spine long — a drinking bird, not a bow.
▶ "single-leg RDL bodyweight"
06

Walking lunges

6 / leg · tall torso · controlled down

knee tracks under load — quad + glute switched on before impact

  • Front knee over the toes, never caving inward.
  • Push through the front heel to stand into the next step.

✕ short choppy steps with the torso tipped forward

▶ "walking lunge form"

4 · Spring — Achilles & calves

~1.5 min
07

Calf raises, slow lower

12 · up fast, down in 3 s · both legs

the 3-second lower is Achilles-tendon medicine

  • Full height on the toes, then lower on a slow count of three.
  • Any Achilles ache: keep them flat-ground, skip the pogos next card.
▶ "eccentric calf raise"
08

Ankle pogos, small

2 × 15 · tiny, quiet, elastic

primes tendon stiffness — free speed, protected Achilles

  • Legs long, bounce from the ankles only, minimal ground time.
  • Quiet = correct. Loud slapping = resting too long on the ground.

✕ doing these cold — always after the calf raises

▶ "pogo hops ankle drill"
09

A-march → easy jog

2 × 15 m march · then jog off into the run

stitches the warm-up into your stride mechanics

  • Knee to horizontal, toe up, foot lands under the hip.
  • Let the second set drift into a light skip, then simply keep jogging.
▶ "A-march drill"

Then the run starts slow: first 10 minutes ≥30 s/km slower than easy pace, HR under ~140. Strides only after 15+ minutes of running — and none on a day the hamstring feels tight.

Tight / sore hamstring day: double the swings and scoops, shorten your stride, skip all strides and hills. A hamstring that stiffens mid-run = stop, walk, Normatec — exactly the right call, not a failure.

Sharp pain anywhere (hamstring, Achilles, knee) = the session is over. Tightness you can jog through; sharpness you cannot.

After the run: 2 min walk, then gentle 30 s static holds — hamstrings, calves, hip flexors. That's where the long stretching lives, never before.
