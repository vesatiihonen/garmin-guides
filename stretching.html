Cooper block · post-run / evening · hamstring · knee · Achilles

Stretch & Mobility

You don't stretch — let's fix that with a short, targeted routine for the exact tissues that limit you: hamstrings (the descent problem), calf/Achilles, and the knee (via quads, hips, glutes). Do it when you're warm — after a run or in the evening — never cold or hard before running. Tap any move for a video.

The routine · ~8 min
  1. Hamstring — supine strap stretch, 2×30 s/side
  2. Calf (gastroc) — wall, straight knee, 2×30 s/side
  3. Achilles (soleus) — wall, bent knee, 2×30 s/side
  4. Quad / knee — standing quad, 2×30 s/side
  5. Hip flexor — half-kneel, 2×30 s/side
  6. Glute / piriformis — figure-4, 2×30 s/side

Ease into each hold, breathe, let it release — never bounce, never push into sharp pain (especially the knee). Mild stretch tension only. Daily is ideal, at minimum after every run.

body pull / direction ✕ avoid

Hamstrings

the descent tissue
01

Supine hamstring

2 × 30 s each leg · strap or towel

Targets: hamstring belly + back of knee

  • On your back, loop a strap round the foot, raise the leg.
  • Keep the knee softly bent, draw the thigh toward you until you feel a mild stretch.
  • Down leg stays long on the floor.

✕ locking the knee or yanking — it grabs the hamstring

▶ "supine hamstring stretch strap"
02

Nerve floss (if very tight)

10 slow reps each leg · gentle

Targets: hamstring that feels "tight" not short

  • Seated: as you straighten the knee, look up; as you bend it, tuck the chin.
  • Glide in and out — this mobilises the nerve, it is not a hard stretch.
  • Use when the hamstring feels tight/guarded rather than genuinely short.

✕ holding it like a stretch or forcing range

▶ "sciatic nerve floss seated"

Calf + Achilles

two angles — do both
03

Gastroc calf

2 × 30 s each leg · back knee STRAIGHT

Targets: upper calf (gastrocnemius)

  • Hands on the wall, back leg straight and long behind you.
  • Back heel pressed down, toes forward; lean in until the upper calf stretches.

✕ letting the back heel lift or the foot turn out

▶ "gastrocnemius wall stretch"
04

Soleus / Achilles

2 × 30 s each leg · back knee BENT

Targets: lower calf + Achilles tendon

  • Same wall stance, but bend the back knee toward the wall.
  • Heel stays down — the stretch drops into the low calf / Achilles.
  • This one directly loads the Achilles — go gentle if it's cranky.

✕ bouncing on a sore Achilles — steady hold only

▶ "soleus Achilles bent-knee stretch"

Knee — via quad, hip, glute

what actually frees the knee
05

Standing quad

2 × 30 s each leg · hold a wall for balance

Targets: quadriceps → offloads the knee

  • Pull the heel toward the glute, knees together, stand tall.
  • Tuck the pelvis under to feel the front of the thigh.

✕ arching the back or letting the knee flare out

▶ "standing quad stretch"
06

Half-kneel hip flexor

2 × 30 s each side · pad the down knee

Targets: hip flexor → unloads pelvis + knee

  • Back knee down, front foot flat; tuck the tailbone and glide the hips forward.
  • Stand tall — feel the front of the back hip, not the low back.

✕ leaning back or over-arching to "feel more"

▶ "half-kneeling hip flexor stretch"
07

Figure-4 glute

2 × 30 s each side · knee-friendly

Targets: glute / piriformis → hip + knee tracking

  • On your back, cross one ankle over the opposite thigh (a "4").
  • Pull the far thigh toward your chest until the glute of the crossed leg releases.

✕ forcing the crossed knee down or twisting the pelvis

▶ "figure-4 glute stretch"

When: warm muscles only — after a run, after the drills/core, or a relaxed evening routine (pairs perfectly with legs-up-the-wall + Normatec). Not as a hard stretch cold or right before running — the pre-run job is the dynamic warm-up, not this.

How: ease to a mild stretch, hold ~30 s, breathe out and let it lengthen, 2 rounds/side. No bouncing. Never push into sharp pain — a gentle pull is the target, and the knee especially should feel free, not pinched.

Priority for you: the hamstring + both calf angles + figure-4 — those three do the most for your descent hamstring, Achilles, and knee. If short on time, do just those.
