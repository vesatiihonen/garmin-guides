Drills + Core

Drills first while the legs are fresh, core after — about 15–20 minutes. Everything here needs only your bands, a jump rope, and bodyweight, so it all travels. Tap any move for a YouTube demo.

body position where you were direction / active ✕ avoid

Drills

5–8 min · every session
01

Skipping rope

2 × 60–90 s · easy bounce · ankles only

  • Balls of the feet, heels never touch down.
  • Quiet landings — barely hear yourself.

✕ squatting and jumping high off the knees

▶ "jump rope drill for runners"
02

Pogos / ankle hops

2 × 15 hops · minimal ground time

  • Legs stay long — bounce from the ankle, not the knee.
  • Off the ground fast, like hot pavement.

✕ bending the knees to absorb the landing

▶ "pogo hops plyometric drill"
03

A-skips

2 × 20 m · rhythmic, light

  • Drive knee to thigh-horizontal, toe pulled up.
  • Strike the ground under the hip, not out front.

✕ leaning back to get the knee higher

▶ "A-skip running drill technique"
04

High-knee march

2 × 20 m · slow, controlled

  • Walk, don't hop — one foot always down.
  • Tall spine, ribs stacked over hips.

✕ rushing it into a jog

▶ "high-knee march drill"

Core

8–12 min · every session
05

Front plank

3 × 30–45 s · one straight line

  • Elbows under shoulders; squeeze glutes.
  • Breathe normally — if you can't, shorten it.

✕ hips sagging (dashed) or piked up high

▶ "front plank proper form"
06

Side plank

2 × 20–30 s each side

  • Elbow under shoulder; push the bottom hip up.
  • Top arm straight up, chest open.

✕ bottom hip drifting toward the ground

▶ "side plank proper form"
07

Dead bug

2 × 8–10 each side · slow

  • Extend opposite arm + leg out low, then return.
  • Exhale as you extend; low back stays flat.

✕ low back arching off the floor

▶ "dead bug exercise form"
08

Bird dog

2 × 8–10 each side · pause 2 s

  • Reach opposite arm + leg to horizontal, no higher.
  • Hips square — glass of water on your back.

✕ twisting the hips open to lift the leg

▶ "bird dog exercise form"
09

Glute bridge

2 × 12–15 · or single-leg / banded

  • Heels close to glutes; drive through the heels.
  • Squeeze glutes at the top, hold 2 s.

✕ arching the lower back at the top

▶ "glute bridge exercise form"

Hips + Adductors

protects the knee · with bands
10

Lateral band walks

2 × 10 steps each way · band at knees

  • Athletic half-squat; stay low the whole set.
  • Small controlled steps — keep band tension.

✕ standing tall and letting the knees cave

▶ "lateral band walk"
11

Clamshells

2 × 12 each side · band above knees

  • Feet together, open the top knee like a clam.
  • Keep hips stacked — don't roll back.

✕ rocking the hips back to open wider

▶ "clamshell exercise with band"
12

Copenhagen plank

2 × 15–20 s each side · short lever

  • Top knee on a chair / low wall / sun-lounger.
  • Squeeze the surface, lift hips to a straight line.
  • Build +5 s a week — never rush this one.

✕ ankle (not knee) on the surface = groin strain

▶ "copenhagen plank beginner"

Order: drills first while the legs are fresh, then core, then the banded hip work. 6×/week, one day off.

Traveling: it's your recovery week — keep the drills light. Do it early or in shade (heat). The Copenhagen surface can be a low wall, bed edge, rock, or sun-lounger.

Safety: adductors strain easily — any sharp groin or Achilles pain means stop the session, not push through it.
